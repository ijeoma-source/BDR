Case Studies

Pipeline built from scratch.

Reviving a 5-Year Dormant Pipeline
AIESEC RWANDA · Operations & Sales Manager
Outbound · Automation · B2C
120+ Inbound sign-ups in 2 weeks
15 Paying customers from zero
50+ Warm leads still in pipeline
$0 Revenue before → $80–$140/customer

Problem

AIESEC Rwanda had not generated paying customers for five years. Existing application data existed but was unusable; no structured ICP, no active pipeline, and a product (paid international volunteer placements, $80–$140) that was being pitched to the wrong audience with the wrong message. An entire market segment remained completely unexplored.

Strategy

  • Cleaned and re-segmented the historical application database
  • Built 3 ICP segments from pattern analysis in prior application data
  • Identified a talent–opportunity mismatch in existing data and repositioned messaging accordingly
  • Used community signal sourcing: Kigali Talents, tech communities, LinkedIn groups

Execution

  • Built a discovery form with behavioral prompts to profile leads before calls
  • Wrote personalized outreach based on community signals; each message tied to specific pain points
  • Automated the post-form nurture sequence: Brevo (email) + Zapier (workflow triggers)
  • Tracked pipeline in Google Sheets: responses, outreach status, calls booked, paid deals
  • Ran a full campaign targeting community members who had expressed interest in international opportunities

Results

  • 120+ inbound sign-ups within 2 weeks of campaign launch (August)
  • 6 paying customers in the first 2 months (first revenue in 5 years from that product)
  • 15 total paying customers 9 additional committed and converted in subsequent weeks
  • 50+ warm leads mapped and nurtured but not yet converted; live pipeline with defined next steps
Learning

A subset of leads dropped off at the form stage due to language barrier form questions written in English were misunderstood by French/Kinyarwanda-dominant prospects. Fix: build localized form versions per language segment, and flag language preference as a qualification criterion earlier in the outreach sequence.

Outreach Samples

Messages that actually convert.

Outreach that works isn't generic; it's signal-based, ICP-specific, and built around the prospect's actual context. Below are real-world examples and frameworks I use.

LinkedIn · Community Signal Outreach Used → AIESEC Rwanda campaign

Hi [Name], I noticed you mentioned in the Kigali Talents group that you're looking to build international experience in [their field]. Most people in your position either wait for the "right time" or miss the window entirely. The people I've seen actually make it abroad don't wait — they use structured pathways. That's exactly what we facilitate at AIESEC: short-term international volunteer placements that give you real cross-border experience + a global network, in [timeframe]. Worth a quick 15-minute call this week?

Signal used: community post indicating interest in international experience. Message opens with proof of attention, not a pitch.
Email · Post-Form Follow-Up Automated via Brevo + Zapier

Hi [Name], Thanks for filling out our interest form. Based on your answers, I can already see why an international placement makes sense for where you want to go. You mentioned [specific answer from form]. That's exactly the profile that fits well with our [Country/Program] opportunity. I'd love to walk you through it in detail and see if it's the right fit. Here's a link to book a 20-minute call: [Calendly link] Looking forward to it.

Personalised using data from the qualification form. Triggered automatically 30 mins post-submission via Zapier → Brevo sequence.

The repeatable process.

01
ICP Mapping

02

03

04
